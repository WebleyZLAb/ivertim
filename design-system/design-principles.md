# Design Principles

1. Prefer clarity over novelty.
2. Prefer consistency over isolated visual brilliance.
3. Prefer whitespace over borders.
4. Prefer borders or surface contrast over shadows.
5. Prefer one primary action per context.
6. Prefer semantic tokens over base tokens.
7. Prefer proof over claims.
8. Prefer real process photography over decorative imagery.
9. Prefer fluid scaling for large relationships and fixed precision for controls.
10. Prefer the simplest solution that preserves quality, accessibility and maintainability.
